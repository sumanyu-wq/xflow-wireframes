Before You Transfer

Key things to know

A quick primer on what shapes your transfer — from rates to regulations.

Start Sending View live rates

Transfer speed

Digital providers deliver in minutes to 1 business day. Bank wires take 2–5 days via SWIFT and RBI clearing.

Exchange rate markup

The real rate is the mid-market rate. Banks add 2–4% silently on top — always check what you're actually offered.

Transfer fees

Some providers charge $3–$15 flat per transfer. Others embed the margin into the rate. Xflow charges neither.

LRS (Liberalised Remittance Scheme)

Indian residents can receive inward remittances without limit. US residents must follow FBAR and IRS reporting rules.

Delivery methods

Funds land via NEFT/IMPS bank credit, UPI wallet, or cash pickup. Bank credit is the most common and widely supported.

Tax implications

Family remittances to India are generally tax-free. US senders may need to report gifts over $18,000/year on Form 709.

Regulations

Regulations & tax considerations

What you need to know before sending — broken down by who it applies to.

🇺🇸
Sending from
For the sender —
United States
KYC / ID requirements in the United States
Government-issued photo ID
US driver's license, state ID, or passport required before your first transfer.
SSN or ITIN
May be collected for tax reporting, especially for higher-volume senders.
US address verification
A valid residential address is required. P.O. boxes are not accepted.
Enhanced due diligence
Large transfers may trigger EDD — source-of-funds documentation may be requested.
Transfer reporting limits
No outbound cap
No US legal limit on how much you can send internationally.
CTR — over $10,000
Cash transactions above $10,000/day are reported to FinCEN automatically.
FBAR (FinCEN 114)
Required if foreign accounts exceed $10,000 at any point in the calendar year.
Gift reporting (Form 709)
Gifts over $18,000/year to one person must be reported — not necessarily taxed.
🇮🇳
Receiving in
For the recipient —
India
RBI regulations
No inward receiving limit
No RBI cap on how much an Indian resident can receive from abroad.
Purpose codes required
Banks assign RBI codes to each transfer (e.g. P0301 for family, P0011 for business).
FEMA compliance
All inward remittances are governed by the Foreign Exchange Management Act. Non-compliant transfers can be returned by the receiving bank.
Tax exemption limits
Family remittances — fully exempt
Money for maintenance, education, or medical care of a relative is not treated as income in India.
Gifts from non-relatives — ₹50K limit
Tax-free up to ₹50,000/year. Above this, amounts may be taxable under Section 56(2).
Business income — TDS may apply
Professional or business remittances may attract TDS. Consult a CA about Form 15CA/15CB obligations before receiving large sums.
Receiving bank rules
NRE account
Freely repatriable. Interest is tax-free in India. Best for NRIs keeping savings abroad.
NRO account
For income earned in India. Repatriation capped at $1M/year. Interest is taxable.
Resident savings account
For non-NRI recipients (e.g. parents). No special restrictions on receiving inward transfers.
PAN may be required
Receiving banks may ask for PAN for large or frequent inward remittances under KYC rules.

Pro Tips

Tips to maximize every transfer

Small decisions add up. These habits can save you thousands of rupees a year.

01
Always compare the rate, not just the fee

A "$0 fee" offer can still cost you more if the exchange rate includes a 3% markup. Always calculate the final INR amount your recipient receives.

02
Send larger amounts less frequently

If you send $500/month, consider sending $1,500 every 3 months instead. You'll save on any per-transfer overhead and reduce processing friction.

03
Use ACH over debit card when possible

ACH bank transfers are free and slower (1 day). Debit card payments are instant but may carry a small processing fee. Plan ahead when you can.

04
Watch for rate volatility around RBI announcements

The USD/INR rate can swing 0.5–1% around RBI policy decisions or US Fed announcements. Timing a large transfer can yield a meaningfully better rate.

05
Set up a repeat transfer with Xflow

Xflow's scheduled transfers let you automate monthly remittances. You always get the live mid-market rate — no need to log in and check manually.

06
Verify recipient details once, reuse forever

Double-check your recipient's IFSC code and account number on the first transfer. Xflow saves them securely so future transfers take under 30 seconds.
